Joanne Kathleen Rowling

Author

Early life and Education

Born: July 31, 1965, in Yate (near Bristol), Gloucestershire, England.Raised in nearby Winterbourne and later Tutshill, she started writing stories at a young age, publishing a tale called Rabbit at age six.Attended Wyedean Comprehensive School, where she excelled in English and History.She studied French and Classics at the University of Exeter, graduating in 1986. Later worked in London for Amnesty International as a researcher and bilingual secretary.

Writing and breakthrough

During a train journey from Manchester to London in 1990, the idea of Harry Potter simply fell into her head, and she began writing the first novel shortly after.Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone was finally published in 1997 by Bloomsbury after multiple rejections; its success ignited a worldwide phenomenon that spanned seven books published between 1997 and 2007 .

Philanthropy and Honours

  1. Founder of the Volant Charitable Trust (2000) and Lumos (2005), focusing on supporting children’s welfare and ending institutionalization of children.
  2. Recipient of numerous awards: OBE (2000), Companion of Honour (2017), honorary degrees from universities including Exeter, Edinburgh, Harvard, and Aberdeen, as well as honors such as the Légion d’Honneur and British Book Awards.
